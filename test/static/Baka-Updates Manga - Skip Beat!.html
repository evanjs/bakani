Wow I've been following this for 11+ years... I'm older than Kyoko now...  
by moonmystery
June 24th, 2020, 6:45pm
Rating: 9.7 / 10.0
My last post for Skip Beat was from back in 2014.... Unbelievable!

What a classic. This series is the DEFINITION of a slowwww burn. No "falling in love overnight" to be found here. The art might not be the best nowadays, but this manga honestly helped me grow up. I didn't have the best childhood/teenage years, but Skip Beat! helped me mature as a human being despite my isolation from a warm family/group of friends. Kyoko is such a tough woman, and it comforted me to see her journey. I remember pondering various themes in the manga long after I read the chapter, just to imagine what I might have done, and how I could improve myself like she always is striving to.

There was a theme of how she empathized with personalities different from hers once she pretended to actually be them. This helped me outgrow my shy self and become a social butterfly in college. Fake it 'til you make it, so they say. It's crazy to think that so many people, myself included, have their lives molded by a monthly comic strip. Such a warm sense of nostalgia... I'm going to tear up ha ha.

It's a commitment.  
by Spinne12225
May 30th, 2020, 9:12pm
Rating: N/A
Skip Beat is a really good manga. It touches on some really dark stuff, doesn't rush the plot, and also has a good sense of humor. The problem is that if you find out that you like it, be prepared to wait for it. I remember seeing this when it was first releasing chapters back in '06, and I decided to wait. Then about 8 years ago a friend of mine convinced me to start reading it, and I didn't check to see whether or not it was done. That was my mistake, because that was 100 chapters ago, and only about 4 months long in the manga time-line, which included several time skips. I've heard rumors of it coming to a close soon, but I expect that in maybe another 15 years at this pace...
The story spends a lot of time focusing on the personal growth of each of the characters. Which is one of the reasons it takes so long, but at the same time makes it so good. The characters have their fair shares of ups and downs and they have their own issues and we get to slowly see them resolving them and working through them.

What's the point to drag on this long?  
by rinaswan
October 4th, 2019, 12:56am
Rating: 9.0 / 10.0
After Garasu no Kamen, I didn't think there would be any manga that could give me the same level of excitement for show biz, but Skip Beat! really is something!!. This manga deserves high praise although it takes longer than necessary as if it will never end, especially in the romantic aspect. Oh, the characters are still young (forever), aren't they? But the readers aren't. LoL

However, I personally love Skip Beat! even more knowing that the male lead is of Caucasian descendant. ♡ The plot is so fantastic concerning the love triangle, the jealousy, the friendship, and the career goal. The artwork is not that bad, except anatomical oddity. Also, it's hard sometimes to distinguish the characters' faces since they look very similar. As a whole, Skip Beat! is a nice shoujo anyone who loves a long read won't be disappointed!
