Boku wa Tomodachi ga Sukunai (Novel)   
Login to add items to your list, keep track of your progress, and rate series!
Description

Hasegawa Kodaka has transferred schools, and he's having a hard time making friends. It doesn't help that his blond hair tends to make people think he's a delinquent. One day, he runs into his bad-tempered solitary classmate Yozora while she's talking animatedly to her imaginary friend Tomo. Realizing that neither of them have any actual friends, they decide that the best way to alter this situation is to form a club and start recruiting.
That is how "Rinjinbu" was formed, a club specifically designed for people who don't have very many friends. As other lonely classmates slowly join their little club, they'll try to learn how to build friendships through cooking together, playing games, and other group activities. But will this group of relationship-challenged misfits really be able to get along?

Generic romantic comedy, until volume 8, where it becomes a compelling story of (un)requited love  
by tameflame
11 years ago
Rating: N/A

I'll be honest, everything up to volume 8 is just simple character development in the midst of humorous yet bland storytelling.

The characters themselves are nothing special, and don't really progress further than cliched archtypes.

That said, this all changes in volume 8,

At the end of volume 8 it is revealed that the MC and all other major characters indeed have a grasp of their romantic entanglements, this isn't just a bullshit harem with weak characters anymore, these girls and the guy are coming to terms with what it is to fall in love, to have a relationship, and to balance friendships with multiple people in the face of adversity and immense jealousy. Characters are revealed to be fundementally distrusting of some of their counterparts.

The story takes a turn from bland, boring, and staple, to nuvaeu and shatters previous conceptions about these characters. No longer are they archetypal, these characters finally begin to become three-dimensional.

And it is with this, that I can confidently say that this is now one of the most well-written and well thought out works out there, and as somone who has read hundreds of volumes of light novels, this one now easily breaks into the top 1% of compelling storytelling and innovative character development
